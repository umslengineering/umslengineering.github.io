ABET Accreditation

Ensuring quality, promoting innovation, and preparing graduates for the global workforce.

Quality Assurance

The Gold Standard of Engineering Education

The Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering (BSEE) at UMSL is designed to meet the rigorous standards of ABET, the global accreditor of college and university programs in applied and natural science, computing, engineering, and engineering technology.

ABET accreditation provides assurance that a college or university program meets the quality standards of the profession for which that program prepares graduates.

Program Educational Objectives (PEOs)

Our graduates are expected to achieve these milestones within a few years of graduation.

Technical Proficiency

Graduates will successfully apply their electrical engineering skills to solve various challenges in industry, academia, or other professional fields.

Professional Leadership

Graduates will achieve leadership roles, work effectively in multidisciplinary teams, and communicate clearly with a range of audiences.

Lifelong Learning

Graduates will demonstrate a commitment to continuously acquiring new knowledge and adapting to technological advancements.

Student Outcomes

Skills, knowledge, and behaviors that students acquire as they progress through the program.

  • Outcome 1: Identify, formulate, and solve complex engineering problems by applying principles of engineering, science, and mathematics.
  • Outcome 2: Apply engineering design to produce solutions that meet specified needs with consideration of public health and safety.
  • Outcome 3: Communicate effectively with a range of audiences.
  • Outcome 4: Recognize ethical and professional responsibilities in engineering situations.
  • Outcome 5: Function effectively on a team whose members together provide leadership and create a collaborative environment.
  • Outcome 6: Develop and conduct appropriate experimentation, analyze and interpret data, and use judgment to draw conclusions.
  • Outcome 7: Acquire and apply new knowledge as needed, using appropriate learning strategies.
