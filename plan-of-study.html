Curriculum Notes

  • Total Credit Hours: 128 (May vary slightly based on elective choices)
  • Global Perspectives: Only 1000-2000 courses in the Global Perspectives area that also fulfill a Humanities/Fine Arts or Social Sciences Explore areas are acceptable. Check with Advisor.
  • Gen Ed Explore, Social Sciences: Includes any 1000-2000 course from subject areas: Accounting, Anthropology, Business Administration, Communication, Criminology and Criminal Justice, Economics, Educational Psychology, Entrepreneurship, Finance, Geography, Media Studies, Political Science, Psychology, Social Work, Sociology, Sport Management.
  • Gen Ed Transfer: Courses could be satisfied by taking Advanced Credit Courses in high school transferring in these credits to UMSL.
  • Gen Ed Communication Proficiency Core: Recommends COMM 1040 – Public Speaking or COMM 2240 – Persuasive Communication.

EE Course Descriptions

Detailed technical breakdowns for our core engineering and mathematics curriculum.

MATH 1800: Analytic Geometry and Calculus I

Prerequisites: MATH 1030 and MATH 1035, or MATH 1045.

Introduction to differential and integral calculus. Topics may include limits, derivatives, related rates, Newton's method, the Mean-Value Theorem, Max-Min problems, the integral, and the Fundamental Theorem of Integral Calculus.

MATH 1900: Analytic Geometry and Calculus II

Prerequisite: MATH 1800.

Covers analytical geometry and additional aspects of calculus. Topics may include inverse functions, integration techniques, applications of integration, parametric and polar equations, and infinite series.

MATH 2000: Analytic Geometry and Calculus III

Prerequisite: MATH 1900.

Topics include vectors, cylindrical and spherical coordinates, vector-valued functions, functions of several variables, partial and directional derivatives, extrema, and multiple integrals.

MATH 2020: Introduction to Differential Equations

Prerequisite: MATH 2000.

Topics include linear differential equations, equations with constant coefficients, Laplace transforms, power series solutions, and systems of ordinary differential equations.

MATH 1320: Introduction to Probability and Statistics

Prerequisite: MATH 1030 or MATH 1045.

Covers basic concepts and methods in probability and statistics. Topics include descriptive statistics, probabilities of events, random variables, sampling distributions, estimation, and hypothesis testing.

ENGR 1414: Elementary Engineering Design

Prerequisites: MATH 1030/1035, or MATH 1045.

Fundamental concepts and processes (project planning, design practices, teamwork, innovation, and systems integration) involved in designing engineering systems. Includes team projects and reports.

ENGR 2022: Engineering Economics and Project Management

Prerequisite: Junior/Senior standing.

Application of economics to engineering and industrial problems requiring a knowledge of engineering for their solution.

ENGR 4400: Fundamentals of Engineering (FE Exam) Review

Prerequisite: Senior Standing.

Review of material covered in the FE exam, the first step towards becoming a licensed professional engineer (P.E.).

EENG 1108: Object-Oriented Programming

Prerequisite: COMP SCI 1250 or equivalent.

Introduction to programming concepts using object-oriented programming (OOP). Topics include classes, objects, inheritance, polymorphism, data structures, and algorithms.

EENG 2310: Circuit Analysis I

Prerequisites: MATH 1900 and PHYSICS 2111/2111L. Corequisite: MATH 2020.

Basic circuit analysis techniques. Topics include Ohm’s Law, Kirchhoff’s Laws, nodal and mesh analysis, Thevenin/Norton equivalents, and sinusoidal steady-state analysis.

EENG 2800: Digital Circuit Design with Verilog/VHDL

Prerequisite: EENG 1108.

Introduction to digital logic circuits and hardware description languages. Topics include logic gates, combinational and sequential logic design, finite-state machines, and FPGA implementation.

EENG 3310: Electronics I and PCB Design

Prerequisites: EENG 2320.

Study of electronic devices and circuit applications. Topics include semiconductor physics, diodes, BJTs, FETs, and small-signal models. Includes SPICE simulation and PCB fabrication.

EENG 3500: Signals and Systems

Prerequisite: MATH 2020.

Study of continuous- and discrete-time signals and systems. Topics include convolution, Fourier series, Fourier/Laplace/z-transforms, and system response.

EENG 3410: Electromechanical Energy Conversion

Prerequisite: EENG 2320.

Electromagnetic principles applied to electromechanical devices. Topics include transformers, DC/AC machines, power electronics, and energy efficiency.

EENG 4410: Control Systems

Prerequisite: EENG 3500.

Analysis and design of feedback control systems. Topics include system modeling, stability, root locus, Bode/Nyquist plots, and PID controllers.

EENG 4108: Artificial Intelligence & Deep Learning

Prerequisite: Senior standing.

Machine learning and neural networks in electrical engineering. Topics include supervised/unsupervised learning, CNNs, RNNs, and applications in signal processing and robotics.

EENG 4980/4990: Senior Design I & II

Prerequisite: Senior standing.

Two-semester capstone sequence. Teams propose, plan, develop, test, and present a significant engineering design project. Includes professional documentation and ethics.

Refer to the UMSL course catalog for a complete list of course descriptions and prerequisites.
